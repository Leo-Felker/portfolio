Noah Kahan

Singer, Songwriter, Artist

About The Artist

Noah Kahan was born on January 1st, 1997 in Strafford, Vermont. He grew up on a tree farm with his Jewish father who taught him to play guitar and his Christian mother who taught him how to write. Kahan started writing songs when he was eight and would upload them to YouTube and SoundCloud. He attended Hanover High School in New Hampshire and was accepted to Tulane University in New Orleans but decided not to attend in order to focus on his music.

Stick Season

"Stick Season" was Noah Kahan's breakthrough album that kickstarted his popularity in the musical world. Kahan started posting verse's from what would later be his hit song "Stick Season" on TikTok in 2020 which helped him gain a large fanbase before his album even dropped. Once his "Stick Season" album was released in 2022 his following continued to grow as the album gained popularity quickly. His "Stick Season" album was a big step away from his pop heavy albums and into something more folk centric. "Stick Season" the album reached number 14 on the Billboard 200 which helped make him such a popular name in the music industry. "Stick Season" won Top Rock Album in 2024 at the billboard Music Awards and was nominated for many other awards.

Earlier Albums

"I Was/I Am" (2021), "Busyhead" (2019), and "Cape Elizabeth" (2020) are Noah Kahan's lesser known albums written and released before "Stick Season" came out.

Stick Season (We'll All Be Here Forever)

On June 9, 2023, Noah Kahan released "Stick Season (We'll All Be Here Forever)" which is a deluxe version of "Stick Season". This album included all the tracks off "Stick Season" as well as seven new songs and an extended version of his song, "The View Between Villages". This album was an instant success, with his song "Dial Drunk" on the album being nominated at the iHeartRadio Music Awards for best lyrics. With the addition of so many new songs in the deluxe version of "Stick Season", many popular artists voiced interest in collaborating with Kahan. This led to "Stick Season (Forever)", Kahan's final version of his "Stick Season" album which contains all the collaborations of his new songs.
